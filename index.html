Where Emissions Come From

Healthcare emissions follow the Greenhouse Gas Protocol. Most organisations measure Scope 1 and 2. Few can accurately quantify Scope 3.

Scope 1 - Direct Emissions

Emissions from sources owned or controlled by your organisation directly.

  • On-site natural gas combustion
  • Anaesthetic gases (up to 1,300x GWP of CO₂)
  • Fleet vehicles and transport
  • Refrigeration and HVAC systems

15%

of total

Emissions from purchased electricity, heat, and steam.

  • Purchased electricity
  • District heating systems
  • Steam systems
  • Water pumping and distribution

Operational Pathways to Decarbonisation

Leading healthcare organisations act across multiple operational fronts. Transformation is achievable when emissions are measurable.

Medical Waste Reduction

  • 30-40% reduction via point-of-use segregation
  • 80% emissions reduction via plasma gasification vs incineration
  • 60% waste reduction with reusable surgical systems

Sustainable Procurement

Supply chain accounts for 80% of total emissions

  • Require science-based targets from suppliers
  • Prioritise regional sourcing
  • Apply lifecycle assessment vs price-only decisions
  • Source from ISO 14001 and B-Corp certified partners

Telemedicine Carbon Impact

  • 0.5-1 kg CO₂ saved per remote consultation
  • 20-30% reduction in facility energy burden
  • 15% reduction in hospital admissions via remote monitoring
